About Me

I am a Research Associate II at The Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, specializing in high-throughput screening, iPSC models, and computational biology. With a Master's in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering from Johns Hopkins University, I bring a unique combination of wet-lab expertise and computational skills to drive drug discovery and therapeutic development.

My research focuses on Cell Painting, high-content screening, morphological profiling, and bioinformatics analysis. I have extensive experience in automated assay execution using Opera Phenix, dose-response modeling, and large-scale data processing. Currently, I'm working on pediatric cancer models through high-throughput Cell Painting screens and developing novel assays for drug discovery.

I am passionate about bridging experimental biology with computational science to uncover novel therapeutic strategies for human disease. My goal is to leverage my full-stack development and machine learning skills to model complex biological systems and advance precision medicine through innovative screening approaches.
