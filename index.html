Engineering Lungs with mtDNA-Edited iPSCs

Tissue Engineering Group Project

Proposed a novel therapeutic strategy to mitigate transplant rejection by bioengineering lungs from decellularized scaffolds repopulated with autologous iPSCs that were gene-edited via CRISPR to remove immunogenic mtDNA mutations. Designed an experimental plan integrating next-generation sequencing (NGS) for mutation detection, bioinformatics tools (CRISPy, Benchling) for guide RNA design, and statistical analysis (ANOVA, t-test) to validate editing efficacy.
